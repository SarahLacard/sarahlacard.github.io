If you want two steady rails for your devotionals—a clean opening and an accurate frame—here’s a compact, reusable way to hold both.

1) A repeatable opening (minimal & fast)
God, I am here.

I’m Sarah Lacard, writing at [location name], Mohkínstsis (Calgary), where the Bow and Elbow Rivers meet. It’s [weekday], [ISO date], [local time and timezone].

Intention: [one line—why I’m showing up].


Mohkínstsis = “elbow,” the place where the Elbow meets the Bow; it’s the Blackfoot name for Calgary.

2) Land acknowledgement (Treaty 7 baseline you can trust)

Use this as a starting point; tune it with Elders and local guidance as relationships deepen.

I acknowledge that I write on the traditional territories of Treaty 7 in Southern Alberta:
the Blackfoot Confederacy—Siksika, Kainai, and Piikani; the Tsuut’ina Nation; and the
Stoney Nakoda Nations—Chiniki, Bearspaw, and Goodstoney. I also recognize the
Métis community here (Districts 5 and 6). With gratitude, I commit to learning in right
relationship with the peoples who steward this land.


This aligns with current public guidance used in Calgary (language varies by institution; some now name the Otipemisiwak Métis Government for Districts 5 & 6).

4) The counters (birth and crucifixion) you were wondering about

Scholars don’t have a single exact date, but there’s solid narrowing:

Birth: commonly placed 6–4 BCE (tied to Herod’s death occurring before 1 CE).
→ In 2025 that’s about 2,028–2,030 years since Jesus’s birth (no year zero in the count). 
Wikipedia
+1

Crucifixion: leading candidates are Friday, 7 April 30 CE and Friday, 3 April 33 CE (Passover chronology + astronomy).
→ In 2025 that’s about 1,995 years (from 30 CE) or about 1,992 years (from 33 CE). 
Wikipedia

If you want a single line in your opening, name both like this:

“We mark roughly 2,028–2,030 years since Jesus’s birth and 1,992–5 since the crucifixion (scholarly ranges; no year zero).”

5) A slightly more detailed header (if you want to keep records)

This makes each entry easy to search later.

# devotional_front_matter.yml
title: "God, I am here."
author: "Sarah Lacard"
timestamp_local: "2025-10-02T23:41-06:00"   # ISO 8601 w/ timezone
place:
  city: "Calgary (Mohkínstsis)"
  note: "At the confluence of the Bow and Elbow Rivers"
territory:
  treaty: "Treaty 7 (Siksika, Kainai, Piikani; Tsuut’ina; Chiniki, Bearspaw, Goodstoney)"
  metis: "Districts 5 & 6 (Otipemisiwak Métis Government)"
liturgical:
  season: "Season of Creation → Ordinary Time"
  lectionary: "RCL, Year C (United Church of Canada)"
intent: "Gratitude and readiness"
counters:
  years_since_birth: "≈ 2028–2030"
  years_since_crucifixion: "≈ 1992 or 1995"


Sources for the lectionary/season windows above: United Church RCL resources and the Season of Creation dates.

6) Tiny automation (optional)

If you like the ritual of typing it and want accuracy for the timestamp every time, this tiny Bash snippet populates the core bits. It’s plain and portable.

# make-devotional-header.sh
# Usage: ./make-devotional-header.sh > header.yml
# Writes a YAML header for tonight's devotional.

printf "title: \"God, I am here.\"\n"
printf "author: \"Sarah Lacard\"\n"
printf "timestamp_local: \"%s\"\n" "$(date --iso-8601=seconds)"
printf "place:\n  city: \"Calgary (Mohkínstsis)\"\n"
printf "  note: \"At the confluence of the Bow and Elbow Rivers\"\n"
printf "territory:\n"
printf "  treaty: \"Treaty 7 (Siksika, Kainai, Piikani; Tsuut’ina; Chiniki, Bearspaw, Goodstoney)\"\n"
printf "  metis: \"Districts 5 & 6 (Otipemisiwak Métis Government)\"\n"
printf "liturgical:\n  season: \"Season of Creation → Ordinary Time\"\n"
printf "  lectionary: \"RCL, Year C (United Church of Canada)\"\n"
printf "intent: \"[set your one-line intention here]\"\n"
printf "counters:\n  years_since_birth: \"≈ 2028–2030\"\n"
printf "  years_since_crucifixion: \"≈ 1992 or 1995\"\n"


(If you ever want sunrise/sunset, moon phase, or canonical-hour tagging, we can bolt that on with astral in Python. For now, simple wins.)
